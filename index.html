Michaela Ruiz

I am a UX/UI Designer interested in finding innovative ways to bridge user needs and business goals.

My Work

Department of the Interior Website Redesign

The Department of the Interior is a U.S. Government Agency that protects and manages the Nation’s natural resources. The goal of this project was to redesign the DOI website for a more intuitive and seamless user experience with a focus on the information architecture.

TicketFair Mobile App Design

The aim of this project was to improve the experience for patrons when purchasing an event ticket, thereby increasing the likelihood that they would utilize the service to attend more events.

Chicago Coalition for the Homeless Website Redesign

The Chicago Coalition for the Homeless is a Chicago-based non-profit that advocates for the end of homelesness on the basis that housing is a human right. The goal of this project was to redesign the CCH website to elicit a personal connection and trust in the user, thereby increasing monetary donations made on the site.

My UX Skills

User Research

My background encourages me to place an emphasis on research. Here are some UX research methods I use to validate my designs: User interviews, card sorting, A/B testing, surveys, site maps, usability testing.

User Empathy

All great design must begin with empathizing with the user. Here are some UX tools I utilize to gain a better understanding of users: Empathy maps, storyboards, user scenarios, user insight statements

Qualitative Interviews

Want to better understand the users needs, goals and frustrations? I find the best way to do so is to just ask! When performing a user interview I am sure to ask open-ended question, utilize the 5-whys strategy, and avoid leading questions.
